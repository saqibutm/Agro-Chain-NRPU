**Project:** AgroChain – A Wheat and Sugar Traceability Solution using IoT and Blockchain
**NRPU Project No.:** 15516
**Funding:** Higher Education Commission (HEC), Pakistan — National Research Program for Universities (NRPU)
**Host Institution:** University of Agriculture Faisalabad (UAF), Pakistan
**Principal Investigator / Team:** *To Be Completed by Project Team*

## Overview

AgroChain delivers end‑to‑end, farm‑to‑consumer traceability for Pakistan's wheat and
sugarcane supply chains using a permissioned **Hyperledger Fabric** blockchain, a **Node.js**
gateway, and a cross‑platform **React Native (Android/iOS)** application. Every custody and
processing event — from harvest to retail — is recorded on a tamper‑evident shared ledger,
and consumers verify authenticity by scanning a QR code.

## Problem addressed

Limited transparency, weight/quality manipulation, adulteration, and weak regulatory
visibility in staple‑food supply chains, with no means for consumers to verify origin or
safety of flour and sugar.

## Solution highlights

- Immutable, role‑governed ledger of supply‑chain events (Hyperledger Fabric v2).
- Consumer QR verification: product journey, lab quality results, and **GPS route map**.
- Lab quality reports and automated **fraud/anomaly detection**.
- **Offline‑first** mobile capture for low‑connectivity rural areas.
- **Bilingual** (English + Urdu) interface.

## Outcomes (as implemented)

- Working chaincode (23 transactions), REST gateway, and feature‑complete mobile app.
- Documentation suite and Google Play release configuration.
- Quality/fraud/KPI/consumer‑trust modules integrated end‑to‑end.

## Impact potential

Improved food‑safety assurance, reduced fraud, stronger regulatory oversight, and increased
consumer trust — with a path to provincial procurement integration and commercial SaaS
offerings (see Impact and Technology Transfer reports).

## Status & next steps

Prototype complete; remaining for production: host the Fabric network + gateway, security
hardening (secrets/TLS/validation), automated tests/CI, and field piloting.
