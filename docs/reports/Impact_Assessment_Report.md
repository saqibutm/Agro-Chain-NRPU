# Impact Assessment Report — AgroChain

**NRPU Project No. 15516** · HEC Pakistan · University of Agriculture Faisalabad

> Quantitative figures (beneficiaries, % fraud reduction, pilot KPIs) are
> **To Be Completed by Project Team** after field deployment.

## 1. Intended impact

AgroChain targets transparency, food safety, and trust in Pakistan's wheat and sugar supply
chains — staples consumed daily by the entire population.

## 2. Beneficiaries

| Group | Benefit |
|-------|---------|
| Farmers | Verifiable provenance; basis for fair pricing/attribution |
| Mills/Distributors/Retailers | Auditable custody; reduced disputes |
| Regulators (PFA/PASSCO) | Real‑time oversight, fraud signals, recall capability |
| Consumers | Authenticity + safety verification via QR |
| Academia | Research platform, datasets, publications |

## 3. Economic impact

- Reduced losses from weight manipulation/adulteration (extraction‑ratio & variance checks).
- Lower dispute/audit costs via shared immutable records.
- Potential premium for verifiably traceable produce.
- *Quantified savings: To Be Completed.*

## 4. Social impact

- Improved food safety and public confidence in staples.
- Inclusivity: Urdu UI + offline‑first for low‑connectivity, low‑literacy rural users.

## 5. Environmental impact

- Digitization reduces paperwork; geotagging enables spatial analytics.
- *Lifecycle/energy considerations of the permissioned network: To Be Completed.*

## 6. Alignment with national & SDG goals

- Supports food security and governance objectives; aligns with SDG 2 (Zero Hunger),
  SDG 9 (Industry/Innovation), SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption/Production).

## 7. Risk & sustainability

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Low adoption | Bilingual, offline‑first UX; regulator mandate path |
| Infrastructure cost | Shared consortium model; SaaS |
| Data quality at source | Lab verification + fraud detection |

## 8. Measurement framework (proposed)

KPIs: # batches traced, % scanned by consumers, fraud alerts raised/resolved, pilot
districts, user satisfaction. Baseline & targets: **To Be Completed**.

## 9. Conclusion

High potential for measurable improvements in transparency and food safety; realization
depends on field deployment and regulator participation.
